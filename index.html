Individual Project - Molecular Energy Prediction

In this individual project, I developed two deep learning models, an artificial neural network (ANN) and a residual neural network (ResNet), for predicting molecular energies within the ANI1 dataset. To encode molecular structures into Numpy arrays, I used the DScribe package, which transforms atomic structures into fixed-size vectors or matrices. I also used Scikit-learn and PyTorch.

Protein Function Prediction Model

During my internship at Pivotal Life Sciences, I worked with DeepFRI, a deep learning model that combines LSTM networks with graph convolutional networks to predict protein function. My role involved using Python libraries like Tensorflow and Biopython to develop complementary functions that increased DeepFRI’s utility to Pivotal Life Sciences. At the end of my internship, all code was handed over to Pivotal’s computational biology team.

Cellular Automata Model

To delve into the dynamics of changing allele frequencies in a population, I worked on a collaborative project where my team designed and implemented a C++ cellular automata model. I used Python libraries seaborn and matplotlib to create insightful visualizations depicting our results. This project not only deepened my understanding of population genetics and computational modeling techniques but also honed my programming skills in C++ and Python and strengthened my ability to collaborate.
